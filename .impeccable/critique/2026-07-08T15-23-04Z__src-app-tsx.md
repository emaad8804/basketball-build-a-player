## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Passes the sniff test — a casual observer would not say "AI made this."** The kill list is nearly cleared: Lucide icons throughout, off-default vermilion, no gradient text, varied box-score-reactive recap copy, disciplined two-face type.

**LLM assessment** — three surviving tells: (1) an eyebrow-kicker (uppercase tracked micro-label) opens literally every screen — half-reads as broadcast convention, but the identical rhythm is clockable; (2) emoji still gets rasterized into the downloaded share PNG via resultLine() ("🚨 LOST IN THE PLAY-IN") — the one kill-list violation left, on the single most important asset; (3) flaw/team/season/result/share screens are still center-stacked single columns.

**Deterministic scan** — CLI: 2 findings, both false positives (the flaw wheel pointer's CSS-triangle `border-left/right: 12px solid transparent`, matched without seeing `transparent`). In-browser detector across 3 app states: **low-contrast 3.0:1 — cream #FAFAF7 on accent #FF5A1F on every primary button (needs 4.5:1)** — independently confirming Assessment A's measurement (2.98:1); `text-overflow` on the player-card attribute labels ("Ball Handling" clips to "Ball Handli…" behind Tailwind `truncate`); `all-caps-body` (48 chars of uppercase body text on landing); `dark-glow` (the accent glow-pulse — intentional juice, kept deliberately); `overused-font` (Geist 96% — false positive: deliberate display/body split, Anton is display-only by design).

## Overall Impression

The redesign delivered its identity: the foil cards, the flaw wheel's menace, and the broadcast playoff feed feel authored, not generated. What's left isn't visual — it's structural: the game forgets your run on refresh, its most important asset (the share card) exists in two competing designs with the weaker one on screen, and its middle act (the season sim) resolves without any of the theater the wheels get. Score lands at 24/40 — good bones, real gaps.

## What's Working

1. **The flaw wheel owns its identity** — separate green→amber→red world, honest odds up front, respin "insurance" copy, blood-red environment vs green relief flash. It's the screen players will describe to a friend.
2. **Rarity-is-the-frame is executed, not just specced** — CollectibleFrame makes the conic foil the literal card edge; grade colors ride the rarity ramp so grade and rarity read as one system across reveal, board, and share.
3. **The playoff feed reads like broadcast** — W/L blocks never color-only, series dot-tracker, score-bug rows, recap lines that react to the box score and the player's flaw.

## Priority Issues

- **[P0] No mid-run persistence.** Any reload — including mobile Safari evicting a backgrounded tab, i.e. the target user's commute — dumps a 3-minute career back to the landing screen. **Why**: the core audience plays in interruptible moments; losing a run mid-playoffs is the rage-quit moment. **Fix**: serialize reducer state to localStorage on every dispatch; offer "Resume your run — 6/9 locked" on load. **Suggested command**: /impeccable harden
- **[P1] Primary CTA fails WCAG AA (2.98–3.0:1).** Cream text on vermilion on every primary button; PRODUCT.md commits to AA at 4.5:1. **Fix**: ink #0A0A0B text on accent buttons (10.1:1, and more broadcast). **Suggested command**: /impeccable polish
- **[P1] The playoff ticker performs to an empty room.** Games append below the fold (mobile page grows to ~2,240px) with no auto-scroll; elimination drama plays off-screen. **Fix**: scrollIntoView on the latest GameCard (reduced-motion aware) or a sticky bottom score-bug. **Suggested command**: /impeccable animate
- **[P1] Reduced-motion promise not kept.** Under prefers-reduced-motion, only the foil/sheen stop; cardFlipIn, legendaryBurst, glowPulse, burnIn, and both 4s wheel transitions still run. **Fix**: global reduced-motion block collapsing anim-* durations + JS gate turning wheel timers into a crossfade. **Suggested command**: /impeccable polish
- **[P2] Two hero cards, and the wrong one is on screen.** The downloadable PNG (9 named tiles, OVR medallion) is the better design; the DOM share card shows anonymous pips + 3 Top Picks. Also: PNG rasterizes emoji and lacks team color in the frame and the fan-made disclaimer. **Fix**: unify on the PNG's design as the on-screen final reveal; draw glyphs, add team color + disclaimer. **Suggested command**: /impeccable polish (share screen)
- **[P2] Locking is irreversible, mis-tappable (37px targets), and duplicated.** The lock buttons repeat the grade grid directly above them. **Fix**: make the grade grid the lock control (≥44px), one-beat confirm or 3s undo toast. **Suggested command**: /impeccable distill (reveal card)

**Casey (mobile, one-handed)**: share CTAs sit in the true thumb zone at 46px — good; but "Spin Player" is top-of-screen in the loop she repeats 9 times, lock chips are 37px, and an interruption is fatal (the P0). Playoffs are 2.7 screens tall and self-scroll nowhere.

**Alex (daily regular)**: ~20s/run of unskippable animation (9× flash+scout, two 4s wheels); no tap-to-settle, no shortcuts (L to lock best, S to spin); his streak trophies live as a 12px caption. Playoffs' Fast Forward / Skip is the pattern to extend everywhere.
